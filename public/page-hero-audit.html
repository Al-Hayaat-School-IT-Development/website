Three Approaches

⭐ Recommended

Option A — Shared Page Hero

Create one shared PageHero component: page title, optional subtitle, optional background color or image. Careers keeps its green full-bleed. Donate keeps its verse. Everyone else uses PageHero.

  • Consistent entry point for every page
  • School Plan finally gets an identity
  • Contact + Admissions upgrade from generic gray
  • Careers and Donate retain their personality
  • Requires rebuilding 3 pages' openings
Option B

Adopt the Careers Hero Everywhere

Use the full-bleed green hero (with background image and large display heading) on all pages. Each page gets its own heading text and accent color variant.

  • Dramatic and brand-forward
  • Strong visual rhythm across pages
  • Loses the uniqueness of the Careers page
  • Donate's spiritual tone feels forced in a green hero
  • Heavy — may feel repetitive on utility pages
Option C

Remove the Careers Hero

Simplify — move Careers to the same plain heading style as the other pages. No full-bleed hero on any page (except About/Curriculum which are already richer).

  • Lower implementation effort
  • Loses one of the best-designed pages
  • Overall site feels less designed
  • Still doesn't fix School Plan's missing header
  • A step backward, not forward
💡

My Recommendation — Option A

Create a shared PageHero component with a title, optional subtitle, and a light off-white or white background. Apply it to the 3 weakest pages: School Plan (needs a title), Contact and Admissions (upgrade from the generic gray component). Let Careers keep its full-bleed green hero, and let Donate keep its Quranic verse opener — both are contextually justified and actually strengthen those pages. About and Curriculum are already strong and need no changes.
